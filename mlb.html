Exploring Relationships Between Performance Metrics

THE LAHMAN BASEBALL DATABASE

The Lahman Baseball Database is a publicly available database containing statistics on pitching, hitting, fielding, team stats, salaries and many more for Major League Baseball from 1871 through 2014.

I was curious about the relationships between different performance metrics and the qualities or characteristics of baseball players with the highest salaries. Other investigations include the correlation coefficients between salaries and other metrics like the number of hits and games played. I also wondered where most of the players went to school.

CLEANING AND AGGREGATING DATA

I collected 7 data sets from the website with over 260,000 records of data. I began cleaning the data, formatting the dates and converting datatypes like strings to ints. I then used NumPy and Pandas to find summary statistics (mean,median,mode,..) of metrics for players with the highest salaries ( > 75th percentile) and those for players with low salaries ( < 25th percentile). Finally, I plotted the distributions with Matplotlib.

MAIN FINDINGS

Players with high salary on average have a higher number of games played between 1871-2015. They also have a significantly higher number of of hits, games played and homeruns between this period. Strong positive correlation between number of wins and hits, 0.892. Weak positive correlation between salary and votes received.

Code is on github here.

Data sets: http://www.seanlahman.com/baseball-archive/statistics/

Tools used: Python (Pandas/NumPy/Matplotlib), IPython Notebook
