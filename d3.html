Visualizing U.S. Flight Performance

DATA VISUALIZATION: U.S. DOMESTIC AIRLINES, 2003-2014

This data visualization explores the performance of the top US. Domestic Airlines from June 2003 to April 2014. I collected the data from Bureau of Transportation Statistics which has various metrics on every airline in the US. I created two graphs, one shows each airlineâ€™s annual average on-time arrivals and the other shows the percentage of delayed flights due to carrier.

EXPLORATORY DATA ANALYSIS

I downloaded the dataset from the Research and Innovative Technology Administration (RITA) which contained over 185,000 records of information on every domestic flight from all carriers from June 2003 to April 2014. Through exploratory data analysis, cleaning and filtering the data in RStudio, I was able to see the top carriers based on monthly average arrivals grouped by carrier names and year. This made it easier to see the main trends.

I shortened the data to show only the 5 most active domestic airlines based on monthly average arrivals (61 records). To show the trends in performance, I plotted percentage of on-time arrival (1 - # arrivals delayed / # total arrivals) against year. The graph shows that there is a drop in performance between 2006-2008 for every major airline. The performance then improves from 2008-2012.

I created another visualization which shows the percentage of delayed flights due to carrier (# carrier delays / # total arrivals) over time.

DESIGN AND VISUAL ENCODINGS

I chose to create line charts for this visualization to show the trend over time for each airline. The chart is implemented in d3.js using lines, circles, legends and colors to enhance the graphical representation of the data. I later added interactivity and animation so the audience can hover over and click on the legends to focus on particular airlines.
