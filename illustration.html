DIGITAL DRAWING

This drawing is inspired by the Erector Set, a brand of metal toy construction sets invented by Alfred Carlton Gilbert in 1913.

This is my fist time experimenting with the three-dimensional software Rhinoceros 3D which I used to model 3D pieces of the erector sets. I then arranged the pieces into unique compositions and assemblages. Finally I made a 2D drawing of it using Illustrator.

Tools used: AutoCAD, Rhinoceros 3D, Illustrator

Figural Projections

Another illustration piece that I did following the one above is this comic-like drawing showing different combinations of pieces of the erector set. I paid attention to the composition and focused on nesting 2D and 3D objects on top of one another. Visually, I wanted to illustrate the strange assemblages of different parts and blur the foreground and background of the drawing.
