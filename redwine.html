Red Wine Exploratory Data Analysis

RED WINE EXPLORATION

The Red Wine dataset contains 1599 red wines and 12 different chemical properties associated with them such as quality, pH, fixed acidity, volatile acidity and alcohol level. I picked this dataset to conduct exploratory data analysis (EDA) to identify the structures, patterns, outliers and relationships in the data.

I used RStudio to load in and investigate the data. I began by observing the structure of the data, the variables and summary statistics in order to get an idea of the questions that I could ask about the data.

My main goal was to determine which chemical properties most influence the quality of red wines by conducting univariate, bivariate and multivariate analysis using various types of graph.

KEY FINDINGS

  • 75% of wines have pH less than 3.4
  • Most wines score around 5.6 in terms of quality (values ranged from 3-8).
  • Quality of wine correlates strongly with volatile acidity and alcohol.
  • As wine quality increases, volatile acidity decreases (high of levels of volatile acidity can lead to an unpleasant, vinegar taste).
  • Strong negative relationship between pH and total acidity.
